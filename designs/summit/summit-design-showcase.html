Layout Showcase · stacked

Fixed top band, larger bottom zone

Use `stacked` when a horizontal sequence or compact framing band should sit above a larger evidence field. The rows follow a fixed 1 : 1.618 proportion across the full canvas height.

01

Top row anchors the frame

The upper band stays concise, but its height is still governed by the layout ratio.

02

Bottom row carries the payload

Charts, tables, or media fill the deliberately larger lower field.

03

Read top to bottom

The fixed ratio creates a stable vertical narrative sequence.

bottom slot

The larger lower field is deliberate

This lower zone can hold one large component or a small pair, provided their combined density still reads as a single bottom field.

Component Showcase · editorial-image-top

Editorial Image Top

Image-first editorial module. The visual establishes tone first, then the body copy explains the point beneath it.

Use when

Hierarchy

Watch out

Alpine materials

Lead with atmosphere, then let the copy explain the evidence.

In multi-column spreads this module works best when the image carries the first visual impulse and the narrative remains deliberately concise.

Component Showcase · flow-horizontal

Flow Horizontal

A left-to-right step sequence for process summaries, definitions, or grouped phases. Best with short copy and 2 to 5 items.
